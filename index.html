Christiaan Neethling Barnard

The man who did the World's 1st Heart Transplant

Here's a Timeline of Christiaan Neethling Barnard Life

  • 1922 - Birth: Christiaan Neethling Barnard is born on November 8, in Beaufort West, a town in the semi-desert region of the Western Cape Province in South Africa.
  • 1945 - Graduation: Barnard graduates from the University of Cape Town with a Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBChB).
  • 1948 - Marriage: Barnard marries his first wife, Aletta Louw.
  • 1953 - Doctorate: Barnard receives his Doctorate in Medicine (MD) from the University of Cape Town. His research focuses on congenital intestinal atresia in newborns.
  • 1956 - Fellowship: Barnard moves to the United States to study at the University of Minnesota under the guidance of renowned cardiac surgeon Dr. Walt Lillehei.
  • 1958 - Return to South Africa: Barnard returns to South Africa and becomes a senior cardiothoracic surgeon at Groote Schuur Hospital in Cape Town.
  • 1961 - Development of Heart Valve Surgery: Barnard performs his first successful open-heart surgery. His interest in heart valve repair and replacement grows.
  • 1967 - First Human Heart Transplant: On December 3, Barnard performs the world's first human-to-human heart transplant at Groote Schuur Hospital. The recipient, Louis Washkansky, survives for 18 days after the procedure.
  • 1968 - Second Heart Transplant: Barnard performs his second heart transplant, and the recipient, Philip Blaiberg, survives for nearly 19 months, gaining international recognition for the success.
  • 1969 - Publication of "One Life": Barnard publishes his autobiography "One Life," detailing his experiences as a heart surgeon and recounting his historic transplant operation.
  • 1971 - Honorary Appointments: Barnard becomes a Professor of Cardiothoracic Surgery at the University of Cape Town, gaining international recognition for his work in the field.
  • 1974 - Innovation in Heart Surgery: Barnard develops a surgical technique for correcting the "parachute mitral valve," contributing to advancements in cardiac surgery.
  • 1977 - "The Donor" Book Release: Barnard publishes his book "The Donor," discussing the ethical implications of organ transplantation.
  • 1981 - Separation of Conjoined Twins: Barnard leads a team that successfully separates conjoined twins, adding to his surgical achievements.
  • 1983 - Heart Transplant on a Child: Barnard performs a heart transplant on a child, showing his continued innovation in the field.
  • 1985 - "Good Life, Good Death" Book Release: Barnard publishes another book, "Good Life, Good Death," addressing issues of life and death from his perspective as a heart surgeon.
  • 1986 - Retirement: Barnard retires from active surgical practice due to rheumatoid arthritis.
  • 2001 - Death: Christiaan Neethling Barnard dies of an asthma attack on September 2, in Paphos, Cyprus.
